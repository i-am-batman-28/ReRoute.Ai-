1. PROBLEM WE'RE SOLVING
================================================================================

When travel goes wrong — flight delayed, cancelled, missed connection —
the traveller is on their own:

- Stuck in a queue at the airline counter
- Frantically searching Google for alternatives
- Calling customer service on hold for hours
- No idea about their rights or compensation
- Cascading mess: missed hotel check-in, next meeting gone, connecting
  flight lost
- Stress, anxiety, wasted time, wasted money

Key stats (for pitch):
- 21% of flights in the US were delayed in 2025
- Average delay cost per passenger: $50–250 (direct) + lost time
- 80% of passengers don't claim compensation they're entitled to
- Disruptions cascade: one cancelled flight can wreck a 3-day trip

The current "solution"? The traveller manually fixes everything.
That's broken.


2. OUR SOLUTION — "TripGuard AI"
================================================================================

An always-on, autonomous AI agent that:
  (a) DETECTS disruptions before the traveller even knows
  (b) RESOLVES them — finds alternatives, rebooks, reroutes
  (c) PROTECTS the traveller — handles compensation, hotel, lounge
  (d) COMMUNICATES — keeps the traveller informed, calm, in control

NOT a chatbot. NOT a search tool. An AGENT that ACTS on behalf of
the traveller — like having a personal travel manager in your pocket.

Name: "TripGuard AI"
Tagline: "Your trip breaks. We fix it. Before you even notice."


3. THE 6 PILLARS (what makes this creative, not generic)
================================================================================

PILLAR 1: PREDICTIVE DISRUPTION RADAR
--------------------------------------
Don't just react to disruptions — PREDICT them.

How:
- Monitor real-time flight data (FlightAware / AviationStack API)
- Monitor weather at origin, destination, and connecting airports
- Monitor airport congestion and historical delay patterns
- Use a simple ML model or rules: "Flight DL302 has a 78% chance of
  delay based on weather at hub + aircraft incoming late"

What the user sees:
- YELLOW ALERT: "Your 4:30 PM flight has a high chance of delay.
  We're already looking at alternatives. No action needed yet."
- RED ALERT: "Flight cancelled. We've found 3 options. Tap to
  confirm your preferred rebooking."

Why it's creative:
- Most tools tell you AFTER the delay. We tell you BEFORE.
- Shows real "AI" and "prediction" — not just API calls.


PILLAR 2: AUTONOMOUS REBOOKING ENGINE
--------------------------------------
When disruption hits, the agent doesn't just suggest — it ACTS.

How:
- Pulls alternative flights from flight search APIs (Amadeus, Skyscanner)
- Scores alternatives by:
  → Earliest arrival at final destination
  → Cost (within budget or airline policy)
  → Connection safety (enough layover?)
  → Seat preference match
  → Airline alliance (for ticket transfer)
- Presents top 3 options ranked by "best for you"
- One-tap confirm → agent "rebooks" (in demo: simulated booking;
  in production: API booking or agent-assisted)

Multi-modal alternatives (the creative twist):
- If no good flights exist, agent suggests:
  → Train (e.g. "Take the 6 PM Shatabdi; arrives 30 min later
     than original flight")
  → Bus
  → Car rental
  → Combination: "Fly to nearby city + train to destination"
- This is what real travel managers do; most apps don't.

What the user sees:
- "Flight cancelled. Here are your best options:"
  Option 1: Next flight (airline) — arrives 9:30 PM ✈️
  Option 2: Train — arrives 10:15 PM 🚆
  Option 3: Fly to nearby city + taxi — arrives 10:00 PM ✈️🚕
- One tap → confirmed → itinerary updated.


PILLAR 3: RIPPLE EFFECT ENGINE (cascade handler)
-------------------------------------------------
A disruption is never just one thing. It cascades.

Flight delayed → miss connection → miss hotel check-in → miss
morning meeting → next day's plans shift.

Our agent handles the FULL RIPPLE:

Step 1: Flight delayed 3 hours.
Step 2: Agent checks: "Will you miss your connecting flight?"
        → Yes → finds new connection or rebooks direct.
Step 3: Agent checks: "Will you miss hotel check-in?"
        → Yes → contacts hotel (or notifies you to call) with
           new arrival time.
Step 4: Agent checks: "Will you miss tomorrow's 9 AM meeting?"
        → Yes → suggests: "Move meeting to 11 AM? Draft message
           to attendees?"
Step 5: Agent updates your FULL itinerary — flights, hotel,
        meetings, activities — everything shifts.

What the user sees:
- "Your delay affects 3 things. Here's what we've done:"
  ✅ Rebooked connection → new flight at 8 PM
  ✅ Notified hotel → late check-in confirmed
  ⚠️ Your 9 AM meeting may be tight → Suggest moving to 11 AM?
     [Move it] [Keep it]

Why it's creative:
- Nobody does cascade handling. Apps handle ONE thing (the flight).
  We handle EVERYTHING downstream.


PILLAR 4: RIGHTS & COMPENSATION AUTOPILOT
------------------------------------------
Most travellers don't know their rights. Most don't claim.

Our agent:
- Detects if the disruption qualifies for compensation
  (e.g. EU261: flight cancelled < 14 days before departure,
   airline's fault → up to €600)
- Detects if the airline owes you meals, hotel, lounge access
- Auto-generates a compensation claim (pre-filled with flight
  details, delay duration, regulation reference)
- User reviews and submits with one tap

Also handles:
- Travel insurance: "Your policy covers hotel + meals for delays
  over 4 hours. Here's how to claim." (or auto-files if API exists)
- Lounge access: "You're entitled to lounge access. Here's the
  nearest lounge: Gate B12, 2 min walk."
- Meal vouchers: "Airline should provide meals. Show this at the
  counter: [voucher / regulation reference]."

What the user sees:
- "You may be entitled to ₹25,000 compensation under DGCA rules.
   We've prepared your claim. Review and submit?"
   [Review claim] [Skip]

Why it's creative:
- Turns the agent from "rebooking tool" into "traveller's advocate."
- Most people lose money because they don't know their rights.
  We fix that automatically.


PILLAR 5: STRESS-AWARE COMMUNICATION
--------------------------------------
Travel disruptions are stressful. The agent should feel like a
calm, helpful friend — not a robotic notification machine.

How:
- Tone adapts to severity:
  → Minor delay (30 min): casual — "Heads up, slight delay.
     Nothing to worry about."
  → Major disruption (cancellation): calm + action — "Flight
     cancelled. Don't worry — we're on it. Here are your options."
  → Cascading mess: reassuring — "We know this is frustrating.
     Here's what we've already fixed and what's left."

- Proactive updates at the right time:
  → Not too early (don't alarm)
  → Not too late (don't leave them guessing)
  → Context-aware: if it's 2 AM, don't send push for a minor
    delay on tomorrow's afternoon flight

- Channel choice:
  → Push notification (default)
  → WhatsApp / SMS (for travellers who prefer)
  → In-app chat (for back-and-forth)

Why it's creative:
- Most disruption tools are transactional. Ours feels HUMAN.
- Shows judges you thought about UX and emotion, not just tech.

4. FULL USER FLOW (step by step)
================================================================================

BEFORE THE TRIP:
1. User signs up / logs in.
2. Imports trip: forwards booking confirmation email, or enters
   flight number + hotel + calendar events manually, or connects
   Google Calendar / TripIt.
3. TripGuard AI parses and creates a trip timeline.
4. Monitoring begins: flight status, weather, airport conditions.

DURING THE TRIP (disruption happens):
5. Agent detects: "Flight DEL→BLR delayed 3 hours" (or predicted).
6. Agent checks cascade: connection? hotel? meeting?
7. Agent finds alternatives: flights + train + multi-modal.
8. Agent sends alert:
   "Your flight is delayed 3 hours. Here are your options:
    Option 1: Stay on this flight (arrive 10:30 PM)
    Option 2: Switch to 6E-302 (arrive 8:45 PM)
    Option 3: Take Shatabdi Express (arrive 9:15 PM)
    We've also updated your hotel check-in."
9. User taps preferred option → agent confirms.
10. Agent updates full itinerary + handles downstream effects.
11. Agent checks compensation eligibility → prepares claim if yes.

AFTER THE DISRUPTION:
12. Trip dashboard shows: what happened, what was fixed, what's next.
13. Compensation claim ready → user reviews and submits.
14. Trip continues smoothly.

6. AI AGENT ARCHITECTURE (the brain)
================================================================================

The agent is NOT a simple "if delay then rebook" script.
It's an LLM-powered agent with TOOLS.

AGENT LOOP:
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  1. OBSERVE: Get latest trip state            │
│     (flight status, weather, time, user prefs)│
│                                               │
│  2. THINK: LLM reasons about what to do       │
│     "Flight delayed. User has connection.     │
│      Connection will be missed. Need to find  │
│      alternative. Also check hotel."          │
│                                               │
│  3. ACT: Call tools                           │
│     → search_flights(origin, dest, time)      │
│     → search_trains(origin, dest, time)       │
│     → check_hotel_policy(booking_id)          │
│     → check_compensation(flight, delay, rule) │
│     → update_itinerary(trip_id, changes)      │
│     → notify_user(message, channel)           │
│                                               │
│  4. RESPOND: Present options to user          │
│     (ranked, with reasoning)                  │
│                                               │
│  5. CONFIRM: User picks → agent executes      │
│                                               │
│  6. LOOP: Continue monitoring                 │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘

TOOLS THE AGENT CAN USE:
- search_flights(origin, dest, date, time_range)
- search_trains(origin, dest, date)
- search_multimodal(origin, dest, date)
- get_flight_status(flight_number)
- get_weather(airport_code)
- predict_delay(flight_number)  ← simple model or API
- check_compensation_eligibility(flight, delay_mins, country)
- generate_compensation_claim(passenger, flight, regulation)
- update_itinerary(trip_id, changes)
- notify_user(user_id, message, channel, urgency)
- check_hotel_policy(booking_ref)
- suggest_lounge(airport, terminal, gate)

Why it's creative:
- This is a REAL agentic AI architecture — LLM + tools + loop.
- Judges see: "This isn't a script; it's an agent that reasons
  and acts." That's the 2026 trend.
